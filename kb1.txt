The Wandering Earth is a story about humanity's effort to save itself from a dying sun. The sun is about to experience a helium flash, which will turn it into a red giant and engulf the Earth. To avoid this catastrophe, humanity decides to turn the Earth into a giant spaceship and move it to a new solar system. The journey is planned to take 100 generations. The story follows a young man who remembers life on Earth before it started moving. He grows up, gets married, and has children, all while the Earth is traveling through space. A major event in the story is when the Earth has to use Jupiter's gravity to slingshot itself out of the solar system. This is a dangerous maneuver that puts the entire planet at risk. The story explores the psychological and social challenges of such a long and perilous journey. It also touches upon themes of survival, hope, and the human spirit. The story has a bleak outlook on the future of humanity, with some stories ending in death and despair. The author seems to have a "survival of the fittest" mentality. Societies based on love and kindness are destroyed by more advanced and analytical societies. The story is more of a philosophical exercise than an immersive story.
