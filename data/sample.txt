I've never seen the night, nor seen a star; I've seen neither spring, nor fall, nor winter. I was born at the end of the Reining Age, just as the Earth's rotation was coming to a final halt.

The Sun and the Moon were fixed in the sky, no longer rising or setting. One side of the planet was baked in eternal day, the other frozen in eternal night. The people of the daylight hemisphere, still holding onto a shred of hope, called it the 'Reining Age.' Those in the night hemisphere, in their despair, called it the 'Great Catastrophe.' Both names were soon forgotten as the Earth Engine project began.

My grandfather was one of the first to join the Earth Engine project. He was a brilliant engineer, and his team was responsible for designing the massive fusion engines that would propel our planet out of the solar system. He told me stories of the old world, a world with a blue sky, white clouds, and a sun that moved across the sky. He spoke of seasons, of the changing of the leaves, of the smell of rain. To me, these were just fairy tales.

My world was the Earth Engine, a colossal city built around one of the twelve thousand engines that dotted the planet's surface. The engine towered above us, a metal mountain that pierced the clouds. Its constant, low hum was the soundtrack of my life. The light from its fusion core was our sun, its warmth our only shield against the cold of space. We were the children of the Earth Engine, born to be its guardians and its pilots. Our mission, passed down from generation to generation, was to guide our wandering planet to a new home, a new sun, a new hope. The United Earth Government, or UEG, was the governing body of our planet-ship. They made the hard decisions, the sacrifices necessary to ensure our survival. My father was a high-ranking officer in the UEG, a man of duty and honor. He believed in the mission, in the promise of a new dawn. He taught me that our journey was not just about survival, but about preserving the legacy of humanity, the memory of a world we left behind. He worked closely with Dr. Liu, a lead scientist on the Earth Engine project, who was a close friend of my grandfather. Together, they navigated the political and scientific challenges of our unprecedented journey.
