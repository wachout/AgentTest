The movie "The Wandering Earth" is based on the same concept as the book, but the plot is very different. The movie focuses on a single event: the Earth's slingshot maneuver past Jupiter. The characters in the movie are also different from the book. In the book, the main character is a man who grows up during the Earth's journey, and his father is an astronaut. In the movie, the main character has a younger sister and a grandfather, and his father is also an astronaut. The movie has a more uplifting and triumphant tone than the book. The book is more philosophical and explores the long-term consequences of the Earth's journey, while the movie is more of an action-packed disaster film. The movie is a slice of somebody else’s story, someone who the main character from the book might have passed on the street. The movie revolves around a single moment when Earth must slingshot past Jupiter and out into the universe. In the book, that is part of the story, but such a very small part that it’s hard to believe an entire movie was created out of it.
